## Key Concepts Defined

### From Neo-Institutional Perspective:

| Concept | Definition |
|---------|-----------|
| **Institutional Transformation** | Evolution of universities, industry, and government into new roles (entrepreneurial universities, enabling government) |
| **Institutional Arrangements** | Formal structures like tech transfer offices, science parks, public-private partnerships |
| **Legitimacy** | Social acceptance and recognition of innovation collaborations |
| **Isomorphism** | Organizations adopting similar structures to gain legitimacy |
| **Path Dependency** | How historical institutional contexts shape current innovation systems |

### From Neo-Evolutionary Perspective:

| Concept | Definition |
|---------|-----------|
| **Co-Evolution** | Mutual adaptation of university, industry, and government over time |
| **Variation** | Generation of diverse ideas, technologies, and organizational forms |
| **Selection** | Market, scientific, and political mechanisms that determine which innovations survive |
| **Retention** | Institutionalization and diffusion of successful innovations |
| **Knowledge Spaces** | Overlapping economic, scientific, and political domains where innovation occurs |
| **Self-Organization** | Bottom-up formation of innovation networks without central coordination |
